Space Launch Data Analysis

Project Description

The goal of this project is to analyze a historical space launch data set to answer key questions about the frequency, costs and safety of space missions. This analysis helps understand trends and patterns in the space industry, providing valuable insights into how it has evolved over time. We use data analysis tools such as Python and the pandas and matplotlib libraries to process and visualize the data.

Guiding Questions

  1. Which organization launched the most space missions in a given year?
    • Identifying the organization with the highest number of launches in a specific year helps understand the competition and activity in the space industry during that period.
  2. How many launches were made per year?
    • Counting launches per year allows us to see the evolution of space activity, identifying years with greater or lesser activity.
  3. How has the cost of a space mission changed over time?
    • Analyzing mission costs over time provides insight into economic trends in the space industry, including possible cost reductions due to technological advances.
  4. What months are the most popular for releases?
    • Identifying the months with the most launches can reveal seasonal patterns or operational preferences in space mission planning.
  5. Have space missions become safer or have the chances of failure remained unchanged?
    • Assessing the safety of space missions over time helps us understand whether there have been improvements in technology and procedures that have reduced the risks of failure.

Tools Used

  • Python: Programming language used for data manipulation and analysis.
  • pandas: Python library for managing and analyzing data structures, particularly useful for manipulating tabular data.
  • matplotlib: Python library for creating static, animated and interactive graphs, used to visualize the results of data analysis.

Steps Taken

  1. Loading and Exploring Data
    • CSV file upload: We upload the CSV file containing the space launch data using pandas.
    • Initial exploration: We explore the first rows of the dataset to understand its structure and content, identifying the relevant columns for the analysis.
  2. Data Cleaning and Preparation
    • Removal of unnecessary columns: We remove the columns Unnamed: 0.1 and Unnamed: 0, which are unnecessary indexes for analysis.
    • Date column conversion: We convert the Date column to a datetime format to facilitate temporal analysis.
    • Price column conversion: We convert the Price column to a numeric format to allow cost calculations.
    • Identification of null values: We identify and count null values ​​in the dataset and display the affected rows to decide how to handle them in subsequent analysis.
  3. Data Analysis
    • Organization with the most missions in a given year: We analyze and graph which organization launched the most missions in a specific year, providing insight into the competition in the space industry.
    • Number of launches per year: We count and visualize the number of launches carried out each year to understand the evolution of space activity.
    • Variation of the cost of a space mission over time: We calculate the average cost of missions over time and graph it to observe economic trends and variations.
    • Most popular months for launches: We identify and graph the months with the highest number of launches, revealing possible seasonal patterns in mission planning.
    • Space mission safety: We evaluate and visualize the ratio of successful and failed missions over time to understand improvements in space mission safety.

Results and Visualizations

  1. Organization with the most missions in a given year:
    • A bar chart was created showing the organizations with the most launches in a specific year, highlighting the leading organization in terms of activity.
  2. Number of launches per year:
    • A bar graph was generated that visualizes the number of space launches carried out each year, allowing long-term trends in space activity to be identified.
  3. Variation of the cost of a space mission over time:
    • A line graph has been produced showing how the average cost of a space mission has varied over the years, providing insight into economic trends in the space industry.
  4. Most popular months for releases:
    • A bar graph was created that identifies the months with the highest number of space launches, revealing possible seasonal patterns in mission planning.
  5. Security of space missions:
    • A stacked bar chart was made to evaluate the proportion of successful and failed missions over time, showing improvements in the safety of space missions.

Conclusion

This project has allowed us to obtain a detailed view of space launches, from frequency and costs to mission safety. Using data analysis and visualization tools, we have been able to process large volumes of information and generate clear and concise visualizations. These analyzes are essential to better understand the evolution and trends in the space industry, offering valuable information for researchers, companies and space enthusiasts.
